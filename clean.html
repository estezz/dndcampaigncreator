The Shadow of Emberpeak

Campaign Setting: Middle-earth (Inspired by Tolkien's world)

Party Size: 4

Character Levels: 2

Number of Sessions: 4

Number of Hours Per Session: 3

Location: The Shire, and the foothills of Emberpeak

Setup

The Peaceful Shire is shaken. A shadow has fallen upon the usually idyllic landscape. A young, erratically behaving dragon, known as Cinder, has taken up residence near the small village of Bywater. Its presence has disrupted the peaceful rhythm of Hobbit life, causing small fires, pilfering livestock, and, most concerning, a growing sense of fear. The local Shire-folk, unused to such fiery threats, are at a loss. Your party, travelers of some renown in the Shire, are called upon to investigate and resolve the issue. You are given the task by Mayor Whitfoot to investigate this matter.

Plot Hooks

The Mayor's Plea

Mayor Whitfoot of Bywater, a stout and worried Hobbit, personally requests your help. He offers a generous reward and promises the gratitude of the Shire. He is desperate and has heard tales of your adventuring prowess. He knows not what to do. His own guard are all too frightened.

The Stolen Treasures

A wealthy Hobbit, Hamfast Gamgee, has had several precious heirlooms stolen from his cellar. He suspects the dragon, Cinder, as the culprit. He is offering a sizable reward for their retrieval and the banishment of the dragon. He is looking for her family rings.

Main Plot Steps

Investigation in Bywater

Arrive in Bywater, a village gripped by fear. Interview the locals – panicked hobbits, a terrified innkeeper, and a few brave (or foolish) individuals who have witnessed the dragon's antics. Gather clues: burnt fields, missing livestock, and tales of a juvenile dragon’s behavior. The townsfolk tell of the dragon's erratic behavior - sometimes destructive, sometimes playful, always dangerous. They have witnessed small fires, the theft of chickens, and the dragon's strange interest in shiny objects. You may also find out that the dragon is acting strangely. It’s not just attacking but hoarding random, seemingly useless items. Investigate Hobbit holes for clues.

A bustling hobbit village, Bywater, with smoke rising from a few damaged houses, and hobbits huddling in fear, a young dragon circling overhead.

Tracking Cinder

Follow the trail of destruction left by Cinder. Track the dragon's movements, which leads the party towards the foothills of Emberpeak, a dormant volcanic mountain. Along the way, the party encounters signs of Cinder's recent activity – scorched earth, half-eaten livestock, and a trail of unusual objects (a lost button, a broken music box, a tarnished silver spoon). The journey is fraught with challenges. The path winds through a small forest where a pack of wolves, driven from their usual hunting grounds by the dragon's presence, lies in wait. Successfully navigating these challenges will provide the party with experience and perhaps an early glimpse of Cinder's lair.

A winding path through a scorched forest, leading towards a smoldering mountain peak, the silhouette of a small dragon visible in the distance.

Encounter at the Lair

The party reaches Cinder's lair – a cave system at the base of Emberpeak. Within, they find Cinder (see Monster Stat Blocks), surrounded by its hoard. The hoard includes not only gold and gems, but also the stolen hobbit heirlooms. The party must now face the young dragon. The fight is challenging, utilizing the terrain to their advantage. Inside the lair, they will find an important item. A broken Elvish music box - the music box has the secret to communicate with Cinder.

A dimly lit cave, the lair of a young dragon, filled with scattered treasure and a small, fiery dragon facing off against brave adventurers.

The Dragon's Dilemma

After defeating or subduing Cinder, the party discovers a deeper truth. The dragon is not acting out of malice, but fear and confusion. Using the information found from the broken music box, they find that Cinder has been separated from its parents. The party discovers that Cinder is lost and disoriented, seeking something it doesn't understand. They can either: (1) Drive the dragon away (2) attempt to befriend the Dragon or (3) Try and find its family. If befriending, they will have to attempt to communicate using the broken music box. Using the music box can help you befriend the Dragon. The party must choose whether to drive it off, capture it, or help it.

A heartwrenching scene inside the lair, with a defeated young dragon looking lost and confused. The adventurers, looking on with a mix of pity and determination.

Main Characters

Mayor Whitfoot

The portly and well-meaning Mayor of Bywater. He is a Hobbit of considerable stature but lacking in courage. He is deeply concerned about the village.

A stout, middle-aged Hobbit, wearing fine clothes and a worried expression.

Hamfast Gamgee

A wealthy and slightly pompous Hobbit, known for his collection of heirlooms. He is furious about the theft.

A Hobbit with a neatly trimmed beard, clutching his hands with worry, his home in the background.

Cinder

A young, erratic, and frightened dragon. It is not inherently evil, but its actions are causing trouble.

A young, slightly disheveled dragon, with smoky wings and intelligent eyes.

Monster Stat Blocks

Cinder (Young Dragon)

Armor Class 14 (natural armor)
Hit Points 45 (6d10 + 12)
Speed 30 ft., fly 60 ft.

STR 16 (+3) DEX 10 (+0) CON 14 (+2) INT 10 (+0) WIS 11 (+0) CHA 13 (+1)

Skills Perception +4
Damage Immunities fire
Senses blindsight 10 ft., darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 14
Challenge 2 (450 XP)

Actions

Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 8 (1d8 + 3) piercing damage plus 3 (1d6) fire damage.

Fire Breath. Cinder exhales fire in a 15-foot cone. Each creature in that area must make a DC 12 Dexterity saving throw, taking 14 (4d6) fire damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.

A young dragon, its scales a mix of fiery reds and oranges, exhaling a puff of smoke.

Generated Magic Items

This simple wooden whistle, carved with Shire runes, allows the user to communicate with animals. Useful for calming Cinder or finding clues in the wilderness.

Value: Uncommon

Cinder's Tear

A small, fire-resistant gem, found in Cinder's hoard. It can be used once to cast the 'Protection from Fire' spell on a creature.

Value: Uncommon

The Hobbit's Handy Pouch

A seemingly ordinary pouch that has unlimited storage capacity.

Value: Rare

Generated Plot Twists

The True Hoard

The 'treasure' Cinder has been hoarding isn't gold, but items of sentimental value. The hobbits will have to decide what is more valuable to the dragon.

Family Reunion

Cinder's parents are discovered to be nearby, and are not as friendly as they seem. The party has to deal with two dragons.

A Hidden Conspiracy

The dragon isn't lost, and the fires it started are intentional. There is a hidden force using the dragon for destruction.
