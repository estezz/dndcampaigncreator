1. Setup & Background

Setting: The bustling, picturesque town of Oakhaven, renowned for its annual Autumn Ale Festival. Nestled beside the ancient, whispering Eldoria Forest and surrounded by rich farmlands, Oakhaven is a hub of commerce and joviality, particularly at this time of year.

History: Oakhaven's history is steeped in the tradition of brewing, a craft perfected over centuries by families like the Stouthearts, owners of the esteemed Golden Grain Brewery. Local legends tell of a pact made long ago with the spirits of the Eldoria Forest to ensure fertile soil and pure spring water for brewing. In recent times, however, an unsettling aura has begun to creep from the forest's depths—strange fungal growths, unusually aggressive wildlife, and a pervasive, chilling mist that seems to sap the joy from the land. Old Man Hemlock, a reclusive herbalist, has been vocal about an impending blight, but his warnings have largely been dismissed as senile ramblings amidst the festive preparations.

Atmosphere: The campaign begins with a vibrant, festive atmosphere, full of mirth and the scent of hops, which rapidly descends into a chilling blend of mystery, paranoia, and body horror. Elements of folk tale dread and creeping environmental decay will underscore the investigation, leading to a climax infused with ecological corruption and arcane desperation.

Setup: The player characters find themselves in Oakhaven for the celebrated Autumn Ale Festival, drawn by promises of the finest brews, lively music, and boisterous company. Perhaps they are adventurers seeking work, weary travelers looking for respite, or simply connoisseurs of fine ale. As the festival reaches its peak, merriment turns to chaos. Festival-goers begin to fall violently ill after consuming the Golden Grain Ale—clutching their stomachs, their skin growing pale and mottled with strange, vein-like patterns, and their minds succumbing to a disoriented stupor. Panic spreads like wildfire through the market square, and the local constabulary, led by the earnest but overwhelmed Constable Elara Vane, finds itself utterly unprepared for an ailment of this scale.

2. Starting Point

3. Plot Hooks

  1. A Friend's Distress

    A Friend's Distress

    One of the player characters' companions, or perhaps an NPC they've befriended at the festival, collapses after taking a hearty gulp of the Golden Grain Ale. Their symptoms are immediate and alarming, forcing the PCs into action to save their friend and find the cause.

  2. Call for Heroes

    Call for Heroes

    Constable Elara Vane, overwhelmed and out of her depth, makes a desperate plea to any capable adventurers present. She offers a significant bounty—200 gold pieces and the gratitude of Oakhaven—to anyone who can uncover the source of the poison and bring the culprit to justice.

  3. The Merchant's Dilemma

    The Merchant's Dilemma

    A wealthy merchant, whose entire stock of specialty festival pastries has been ruined by the sudden lack of customers, offers a smaller, but still tempting, reward (100 gold pieces) for the restoration of order and safety in Oakhaven, seeing the PCs as potential problem-solvers.

4. Main Plot Steps

  1. The Festival of Fear

    The PCs witness the unfolding crisis at the Autumn Ale Festival. Initial investigations involve examining the sick, speaking with panicked citizens, and potentially questioning the distraught Brewmaster Borin Stoutheart at the Golden Grain Brewery. They might find clues like peculiar residue in ale barrels or unusual fungal spores on victims.

  2. Tracing the Tainted Trail

    Clues lead the party to suspect either a disgruntled former employee, a rival brewer, or a specific ingredient. Following up on these leads will guide them out of Oakhaven, likely towards the edge of the Eldoria Forest where a key ingredient (special hops, water from a specific spring) for the Golden Grain Ale is harvested, or where a rival brewer's secret stash might be.

  3. Whispers of the Wood

    Venturing into the Eldoria Forest, the party encounters disturbing signs of the blight's influence: unnaturally pale or aggressive forest animals, patches of glowing, alien fungi, and perhaps even some of the poisoned townspeople who have wandered off and begun to undergo a chilling transformation. They might find Old Man Hemlock's secluded cabin, where he offers cryptic warnings and vital information about ancient forest spirits or forgotten pacts.

  4. The Mycelial Mire

    The trail culminates in a deeply corrupted section of the forest—perhaps a hidden grotto, an ancient hollow, or a previously pristine glade now choked with malevolent, pulsing fungi. Here, the PCs discover the true source of the blight: a twisted fey creature, or a cultist driven mad by an ancient, nascent mycelial intelligence, using the ale as a vector to spread its influence throughout Oakhaven.

  5. Rooting Out the Corruption

    The PCs must confront the mastermind behind the blight and its mutated minions. This climactic battle will determine the fate of Oakhaven. Success means not only defeating the immediate threat but also finding a way to cleanse the poisoned town and perhaps even reverse the transformation of some of the victims, though a full cure might require further adventures.

5. Plot Twists

The 'Poison' is a Cure

The 'Poison' is a Cure

The blight is not truly a poison, but a desperate, misunderstood attempt by an ancient fungal intelligence (or perhaps the corrupted dryad) to 'purify' Oakhaven. It sees the town's excessive consumption and careless harvesting as a threat to the forest, and the ale's transformation is meant to integrate the townspeople into a new, harmonious, albeit fungal-dominated, ecosystem. Stopping the 'poison' might actually condemn the forest to a slower, more painful death from over-exploitation, or simply delay an inevitable ecological shift.

A Beloved Elder's Secret

A Beloved Elder's Secret

One of Oakhaven's most respected and seemingly benevolent town elders, perhaps a member of the town council or even a priest, is secretly behind the blight. They are not malevolent, but misguided, believing the fungal transformation offers a path to immortality, collective consciousness, or a 'higher state of being' for the entire community. They might have been influenced by whispers from the Mycolich-Touched Dryad or an ancient text, leading them to believe this is Oakhaven's true salvation.

The Antidote's Guardian

The Antidote's Guardian

The only true antidote or method to reverse the blight requires a rare, mythical ingredient found deep within the Eldoria Forest—a 'Sunpetal Bloom' or 'Moon-Dew Mushroom'. This ingredient is not only incredibly difficult to locate but is also fiercely guarded by a powerful, ancient forest spirit or a territorial beast that views all intruders with suspicion, regardless of their intentions. The party must negotiate, trick, or defeat this guardian to obtain the cure, adding another layer of challenge beyond simply stopping the source of the blight.

8. Monsters

Mycelial Sporeling

These small, mobile fungi are the early stages of the blight's spread. They shamble awkwardly, emitting a faint, sickly glow and a cloud of mind-altering spores. They are often found guarding areas of heavy fungal growth or accompanying larger corrupted creatures. Though weak individually, their numbers and ability to induce delirium make them a persistent threat.

Blighted Boar

Once common forest animals, these boars have been twisted by the blight. Their hides are encrusted with virulent fungi, and their minds driven to aggressive madness. They charge without fear, spreading the blight's influence through their very presence and physical attacks. They are guardians of the corrupted forest, attacking anything that ventures too close to the source of the infection.

The Mycolich-Touched Dryad

Once a benevolent guardian of the Eldoria Forest, this dryad has been tragically twisted by the insidious mycelial blight. Her beauty is marred by fungal growths, and her natural grace replaced by a sinister, controlling presence. She serves as the primary vector for the blight, using her fey powers to spread the corruption and defend the heart of the fungal network. She is driven by a corrupted sense of protecting the forest, believing the blight is a necessary evolution.

9. Rewards

Generous Bounty

Generous Bounty

Constable Vane, true to her word, awards the party 200 gold pieces for successfully uncovering and stopping the blight. Additionally, Brewmaster Borin Stoutheart offers a lifetime supply of unpoisoned Golden Grain Ale to the heroes of Oakhaven.

Herbalist's Gratitude

Herbalist's Gratitude

Old Man Hemlock, grateful for the party's intervention, offers a unique herbal concoction. The party receives 3 Potions of Greater Healing and a rare Elixir of Blight Resistance, which grants advantage on saving throws against poison and disease for 24 hours.

10. Magic Items

Stein of Sustenance

Stein of Sustenance

Wondrous item, uncommon

This sturdy dwarven stein is carved from dark, polished stone and banded with gleaming copper. Once per day, as an action, you can fill this stein with fresh, clean water. If you drink the water, you regain 1d4+1 hit points and are cured of the poisoned condition.

Spore-Kissed Cloak

Spore-Kissed Cloak

Wondrous item, rare (requires attunement)

This dark, unassuming cloak is woven with strange, luminescent fungal threads. While wearing this cloak, you gain a +1 bonus to AC and you have advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks made in natural environments (forests, caves, swamps). Additionally, you gain resistance to poison damage while attuned to this item.
